Downgrading drone firmware

From AR Drone Wiki

Jump to: navigation, search

How to downgrade Drone firmware from 1.10.10 to 1.7.11 for your AR Drone 1.0 (how to manually downgrade the drone firmware if after an upgrade, issues arise such as discussed in the topic above: poor motor performance): http://www.ardrone-flyers.com/forum/viewtopic.php?f=7&t=2713&p=19803&hilit=downgrade#p19803

!!!!!!!!TAKE YOUR OWN RISK STARTING FROM HERE !!!!!!!!!!!!

======================================================

1. Go downloads the firmware version 1.7.11 for AR Drone 1.0 from http://netload.in/datei8vyvmELrN2/Firmware_ar_drone_1.7.11.zip.htm The file you just downloaded should be named with "ARDRONE_UPDATE_PLF". It is actually the image file of firmware 1.7.11. The MD5 Checksum (ardrone_update.plf) = 2bfe82f2022e4c59337bf801bb1c70d9

2. Connect to your Drone's WiFi Network. Use FTP to upload the 1.7.11 firmware to your Drone.

Your Computer # ftp 192.168.1.1 (it may takes up to 20 seconds to prompt up)

  • Connected to 192.168.1.1.
  • 220 Operation successful
  • Name (192.168.1.1: root): (<<--- just press on empty. Null password too.)
  • 230 Operation successful
  • Remote system type is UNIX.
  • Using binary mode to transfer files.
  • ftp> bin
  • 200 Operation successful
  • ftp> put ardrone_update.plf
  • ftp> bye

3. Now, telnet to the Drone and go move the image file ARDRONE_UPDATE.PLF that you just uploaded to the right location for proceeding the downgrade upon next reboot cycle.

Your Computer # telnet 192.168.1.1 (it may takes up to 20 seconds to prompt up)

  • Trying 192.168.1.1...
  • Connected to 192.168.1.1.
  • Escape character is '^]'.
  • BusyBox v1.14.0 (2011-12-13 10:35:27 CET) built-in shell (ash)
  • Enter 'help' for a list of built-in commands.
  • # mv /data/video/ardrone_update.plf /update/


4. Now, resetting the firmware flag file to 0.0.0. So that the downgrade/upgrade can be proceeded automatically during the next boot up process.

  • # echo "0.0.0" > /update/version.txt
  • # echo "0.0.0" > /firmware/version.txt

5. Alright, go reboot it for flashing the 1.7.11 firmware now. Disconnect & reconnect your battery. If disconnecting and reconnecting the battery doesn't trigger the re-flash process. Then try to press on the Reset button (as reported by some users it works). During the boot up. The Drone would turn its LED to Red for about 5-10 minutes. Do not interrupt the battery source in this phase. Otherwise, your Drone could be end-of-life (You have to ship it back to Parrot for the fix probably). Stay still, be patient, don't rush, spend the time for a drink would be nice. Till the LEDs turns Green. Then you may reboot it once again for the normal operation.

6. It is done. Your Drone is now happy again with 1.7.11. No Lagging!
